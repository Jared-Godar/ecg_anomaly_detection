# Historical asset attribution inventory

This inventory records the attribution status of every file in `archive/original_2022/images/`
as of the audit described in [`PROVENANCE.md`](PROVENANCE.md). It exists to make attribution
status transparent, not to change or replace any archived artifact — see the archive
[preservation policy](README.md#preservation-policy).

Do not treat "Attributed" below as a license grant. Per [`NOTICE.md`](../../NOTICE.md), none of
these assets are covered by this repository's MIT license regardless of attribution status.

## Status definitions

- **Attributed** — a source is identifiable with reasonable confidence: from citations present in
  the surrounding notebook text, from the asset's well-known origin, or from a confirmed match
  (an exact file match, or a distinctive visual match to a published appearance) established by
  later research and dated inline.
- **Author-original** — the asset was very likely generated by the original project author from
  data or code also present in the archive, rather than sourced from a third party.
- **Unresolved** — no source could be established. The asset is retained as historical material
  with its provenance explicitly undocumented, per the archive's non-goal of concealing gaps.

## Summary

- 16 files audited.
- 9 attributed to a specific, citable external source: 8 to Christopher Olah's "Understanding LSTM
  Networks" (`RNN1.png`–`RNN4.png`, `LTSM1.png`, `LSTM2.png`, `gate.png`, and `RNN5.png` — the last
  resolved 2026-07-19 by an exact-hash match), and 1 (`watch.jpeg`) to its published appearance in a
  2021 How-To Geek article (resolved 2026-07-19).
- 2 assessed as author-original.
- 5 remain unresolved after a dedicated provenance research pass on 2026-07-19 (`ecg.jpeg`,
  `clinic.jpeg`, `labelec_ecg.png`, `thanks.png`, `fft.png`): each was investigated and no source
  could be confirmed to this inventory's evidence standard, with the attempt recorded per file
  above. Provenance is disclosed as undocumented rather than guessed at.

the earlier "7 unresolved" state and are due a follow-up count refresh). It does not change the
reuse guidance in `NOTICE.md`: unresolved and attributed third-party assets alike remain excluded
from the repository's MIT license and must not be reused in new publications or generated
documentation.
